About me

PROFESSIONAL PATH

Before I began my journey into coding, I’ve always thought about the best possible way to learn how to do so since I started working in our family business (about 3 years ago). I began learning to code on my own many years ago from websites such as codecademy, but ended up having to drop it when I began working in the family business. I witnessed my friends go through coding bootcamps and land jobs that I thought could only be for people with computer science degrees.

I knew my passion belonged within building, disassembling and analyzing things, and I knew that this was one of the main reasons that I decided to pursue music production when I went to college. After two years or so of working with the family business, I was fortunate enough to land an internship at a recording studio in Los Angeles. I worked with them for about a year and decided to quit after I felt that I had learned everything I could from them.

Fast forward another year, and I finally realized that I didn’t want to continue to feel like I’m just grinding through my life so I decided to pursue something that I had a passion for. Coding. Where building, disassembling and analyzing were all inclusive. With the basics of JavaScript learned, I decided to attend a coding bootcamp to fill in the gaps within my knowledge of coding.

My passion was truly tested. I gave up my life as I knew it and spent 800+ hours coding over a period of 3 months covering several languages such as ReactJS, PHP, mySQL and etc. While I can clearly remember how stressful and frustrating it was when certain problems seemed impossible to solve, the feeling when you find a solution to it outweighs it all.

These were the moments I knew that this was exactly what I wanted to do. Solving problems has never been more exciting, and everytime I encounter one,

I embrace it.
